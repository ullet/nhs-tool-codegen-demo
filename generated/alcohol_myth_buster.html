Alcohol myth buster

"Women are affected by alcohol to the same degree as men."

Myth 1/12

Alcohol myth buster

The truth is...

Men have a higher average total body water content than women (62% compared to 52%), meaning men are better able to dilute alcohol than women. Other factors have also been reported that can make women more sensitive to alcohol, such as enzyme differences. All these factors mean that women are more at risk than men from the same level of drinking.

Alcohol myth buster

"Drinking water can lesson the effects of a hangover."

Myth 2/12

Alcohol myth buster

The truth is...

While food and water may ease some of the symptoms, they won't cure a hangover. The best way to avoid one is to moderate your drinking and have water between alcoholic drinks. Remember that water won't make you any less drunk or protect your liver.

Alcohol myth buster

"A cold shower, fresh air or hot coffee will sober someone up."

Myth 3/12

Alcohol myth buster

The truth is...

You might feel less sleepy, but only time will get alcohol out of your body. Depending on your weight, it takes about one hour to process one unit of alcohol.

Myth 4/12

Alcohol myth buster

The truth is...

There can be almost 200 calories in a large glass of red wine. And any sugar in mixers or cocktails comes on top of the alcohol content of the spirits. Alcohol also reduces our self-control, making it easy to eat too much.

Alcohol myth buster

"Alcohol is a stimulant."

Myth 5/12

Alcohol myth buster

The truth is...

Alcohol is actually a depressant. Initially, you may feel more energetic or cheerful because alcohol depresses your inhibitions. However, that means you can also be less able to control your emotions or reactions.

Alcohol myth buster

"Lining your stomach with a big meal before drink can help to reduce the risk of getting drunk."

Myth 7/12

Alcohol myth buster

The truth is...

Drinking on a full stomach before you go out will delay alcohol getting into your system, not prevent it. A meal will only delay the rate of alcohol absorption, but if you go on to drink heavily you will get drunk. However, it's still best to eat a proper meal before a night out, especially foods rich in carbohydrates and proteins.

Alcohol myth buster

"Switching between beer, wine, and spirits will make you more drunk."

Myth 8/12

Alcohol myth buster

The truth is...

Your blood alcohol content is what determines how drunk you are. Mixing drinks may make you sicker by upsetting your stomach, but not more intoxicated.

Alcohol myth buster

"Your body develops a tolerance to alcohol, so you can safely drink more."

Myth 9/12

Alcohol myth buster

The truth is...

The more you drink the more damage your body will sustain and the greater the risks become. Tolerance can actually be seen as a warning sign that your body has started to be affected by alcohol.

Alcohol myth buster

"Drinking more than a glass of wine a day may reduce your chances of getting pregnant."

Myth 10/12

Alcohol myth buster

The truth is...

Women who drink a lot find it more difficult to conceive. A study reported by the British Medical Journal found that as few as five drinks every week may decrease a woman's chance of becoming pregnant. If you want to conceive, it's probably best to avoid alcohol completely.

"White wine is a good choice for a person who wants a light drink with less alcohol."

Myth 11/12

Alcohol myth buster

The truth is...

A glass of white or red wine, a bottle of beer and a shot of whiskey or other distilled spirits can all contain equivalent amounts of alcohol, depending on actual drink size and strength and will give similar readings on a breathalyzer.

The truth is...
